Adam Fein
2022 GD MFA
Politics of Non-Representation, 5/2021

Printed Media

The Politics of Non-Representation is a two-sided broadsheet poster that imagines blank spaces within graphic design as—not empty but—vitally alive portals through which marginalized persons can disrupt, annotate, or trouble dominant logics. The work includes an essay contrasting postcolonial thinker Gayatri Spivak, critical designer Jan Van Toorn, and Modernist designers. And the poster explores two visual ways of marking blank-ness: the intentionally blank page, refigured as a space reserved for future marginal speech; and the anti-collage, a layered dis-assemblage of existing visual material that evokes absence.

Adam Fein
2022 GD MFA
Times Neutral Roman

Video

Times Neutral Roman (TNR) is an experiment in refusing gendered language. Whenever a word or adjective phrase that references a particular sex or social gender is written, the typeface will automatically substitutes an unspecified marker (an X’d rectangle). The marker is a small gap in language—a portal—that makes the writer aware of the use of gendered language, which may be composed instinctively, without reflective deliberation.
