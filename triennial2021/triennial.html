A passage, a window, an entrance, an exit, a threshold, a transition: it is through these portals that the RISD community experienced the past three years. We moved between physical and virtual spaces, projected ours and others’ voices throughout nationwide social and racial unrest, and oscillated between reflections on the past and present, and speculations about new futures. We entered social spaces, classrooms, and work environments through the lenses of TikToks, tweets, and tiny video grids. We sustained epistemological reckonings and “new normals’’ as the separations between home/work, public/private blurred together. The 2021 GD Triennial is an exhibit of works produced within RISD’s Graphic Design Department over the last three years, as they sit at the intersections of disciplines, visions, and practices. Portals enable both transportation and transformation— as visual communicators, we open portals into new ways of disrupting and educating. And as we travel through and across portals—from a volatile past towards a hazy, but hopeful, future—we must embrace the uncertain, constantly iterative process of that transition. Against a background of profound social, technological, and ecological change, our recent efforts have created new portals and our continued vigor and vigilance will ensure they remain open. This exhibition is a testament to the ever-evolving state of design and its endless potential: a pathway to the many worlds we’ve been to before and a portal to the infinite places we will eventually go.
